## VA Instructions (if volume exceeds what Daniel can handle)

### Setup
- Grant VA access to Daniel's LinkedIn account (use a password manager, not plaintext)
- VA monitors comment notifications every 2 hours during business hours (8am-6pm UK)

### VA Response Flow
1. New comment with trigger word (SCANNER, SCORE, SCAN, YES, INTERESTED)
2. Check their LinkedIn profile: job title, company, industry, company size
3. Reply with Step 1 qualify question
4. When they respond:
   - Business owner: send link + log in CRM (`crm_add_contact` with source="linkedin")
   - Agency/consultant: tag and flag for Daniel to respond personally
5. After 24h with no response to qualify question: send the link anyway with a friendly note

### What the VA does NOT do
- Never book calls directly (Daniel confirms all calls)
- Never discuss pricing
- Never send the partnership pitch to agencies (Daniel handles personally)
- Never make claims about results or savings (stick to the script)

## Campaign Calendar

### Week 1 (launch week)
- Monday: Post 1 (scanner announcement) + 1-min video
- Wednesday: Engage with all comments, send scanner links
- Friday: Share 2-3 interesting results (anonymised) as a story

### Week 2
- Tuesday: Post 2 (aggregate insights from real scans)
- Thursday: Post 3 (educational, soft CTA)

### Week 3
- Monday: New lead magnet teaser (ROI calculator or content generator)
- Wednesday: Case study post (first client result, anonymised if needed)

### Ongoing
- 2 posts per week minimum
- Respond to all comments within 4 hours
- Monthly: review metrics, adjust post style based on what's converting

---

## Notes

- The comment-and-DM approach is deliberate: LinkedIn's algorithm rewards comments heavily. More comments = more reach = more leads.
- Don't just post the link publicly. The "comment to get it" mechanic drives engagement AND lets you qualify leads before sharing.
- Always check if the commenter is a business owner or agency. Agencies get the partnership conversation, not the scanner link.
- Morningside's key insight: the lead magnet IS the product demo. People using the scanner are experiencing what you build. That's the "holy shit" moment.
