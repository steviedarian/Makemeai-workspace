# ForeShiloh Discovery Call Script

> The complete sales conversation framework. Print this, have it on your phone, or memorise the flow. Every client conversation follows this structure.

---

## Before the Call

- **Research them first.** Run `/demo scanner` on their website. Know their Google rating, social presence, and obvious gaps before you speak.
- **Have the NDA ready.** Offer it in the first 5 minutes.
- **Have the Project Nanda stat ready.** "95% of AI projects fail. Here's why we're different."
- **Never explain the tech.** You're selling pain relief, not pills.

---

## The Flow

```
Open (2 min) > NDA (1 min) > Discovery (20-30 min) > Transition (2 min) > Pitch (5 min) > Close (5 min)
```

---

## 1. OPEN (2 minutes)

**Goal:** Build rapport, set the frame, get permission to ask questions.

> "Thanks for taking the time. Before we get into anything, I want to understand your business properly. I'm not here to sell you anything today. I just want to understand where you're at, what's working, what's not, and whether there's something I can genuinely help with. If there is, great. If not, no hard feelings. Sound fair?"

**Why this works:** Removes sales pressure. They relax. You're the consultant, not the salesperson.

---

## 2. NDA (1 minute)

**Goal:** Build trust instantly. Tyler's play -- offer it before they ask.

> "Actually, before we go any further -- I've already drafted a mutual NDA for both our sakes. Anything you share with me stays between us, and vice versa. Want me to send it over now so you're covered?"

**Why this works:** Nobody does this. It shows professionalism and immediately signals you're serious about their data. Every prospect relaxes after this.

---

## 3. DISCOVERY (20-30 minutes)

This is the core. Three phases: understand the business, find the pain, quantify the cost.

### Phase A: Understand the Business (5-7 minutes)

**Goal:** Map their world before finding problems. These questions build the context layer.

1. **"Tell me about your business. What do you do, and who do you do it for?"**
   - Let them talk. Don't interrupt. They'll reveal more than you expect.

2. **"How is the business structured? How many people, and who does what?"**
   - You're looking for: bottlenecks, single points of failure, overloaded people.

3. **"Walk me through a typical week. What does Monday look like? What about Friday?"**
   - This reveals the REAL workflow, not the one on their website.

4. **"What are you personally spending most of your time on right now?"**
   - The owner's time is the most expensive resource. Where is it going?

5. **"How do you currently get new customers?"**
   - Reveals their sales pipeline (or lack of one).

6. **"What software and tools do you use day to day?"**
   - Reveals tech stack, integration opportunities, and how manual things are.

### Phase B: Find the Pain -- Tyler's 5 Questions (10-15 minutes)

**Goal:** Identify the top 3 pain points with emotional weight and financial cost.

> "Now I want to dig into the stuff that's actually causing you problems. Every business has things that keep the owner up at night. Let's talk about those."

**For EACH pain point (do at least 3):**

7. **"What is the single biggest problem in your business right now?"**
   - If they say "I don't know" -- ask: "What do you have nightmares about? What's the thing you dread doing every Monday morning?"
   - Small businesses (under 20 people) always know. They live it every day.

8. **"How much is that costing you?"**
   - Push for BOTH time and money: "How many hours a week does this eat? And roughly, what's that costing you per month in lost revenue, wasted wages, or missed opportunities?"
   - If they can't give a number: "If you had to guess -- is it closer to hundreds, thousands, or tens of thousands per month?"

9. **"How long have you had this problem?"**
   - The longer the pain, the more urgent the fix. "Months? Years?"
   - Follow up: "What have you tried so far to fix it?"

10. **"What does the business look like in 12 months if you DON'T fix this?"**
    - This is the consequences question. Makes the pain vivid and future-focused.
    - "Does it get worse? Do you lose people? Does growth stall?"

11. **"If money and technology weren't a problem -- if you had a magic wand -- what would the perfect solution look like?"**
    - This tells you exactly what they want. Now you know what to build.
    - "Don't worry about whether it's possible. Just describe your ideal world."

**Then repeat for pain points 2 and 3:**

> "That's really helpful. What's the second biggest thing causing you pain?"

### Phase C: Go Deeper -- Power Questions (5-10 minutes)

**Goal:** Extract the gold that makes your automations 10x better than anyone else's. These questions go beyond Tyler's framework.

12. **"What's the most repetitive task in your business -- the thing you or your team does every single day that feels like groundhog day?"**
    - This is your task audit entry point. Often different from their "biggest problem" because they've normalised it.

13. **"If you could clone yourself, what would your clone spend all day doing?"**
    - Reveals what they WANT to do but can't because they're stuck in operations.

14. **"What information do you wish you had but don't? What questions can't you currently answer about your own business?"**
    - Reveals data gaps. "I don't know which of my marketing channels actually works." "I can't tell you our actual profit margin per project." This drives the reporting and data modules.

15. **"When was the last time something fell through the cracks? A lead that went cold, a customer complaint that got missed, an invoice that didn't go out?"**
    - Concrete examples of pain. These become your demo stories.

16. **"What's your biggest bottleneck for growth? If you wanted to double the business, what's the first thing that would break?"**
    - Reveals scalability issues. Often it's the owner themselves.

17. **"How do you handle it when you're sick, on holiday, or just need a day off? Does the business run without you?"**
    - This is the AIOS KPI: away-from-desk autonomy. If the answer is "it doesn't run without me" -- that's the sell.

18. **"What does your competition do better than you? Be honest."**
    - Reveals positioning gaps and competitive pressure.

19. **"If I could fix just ONE thing in the next 30 days that would make the biggest difference to your business, what would it be?"**
    - Forces them to prioritise. This becomes your Exploration Month deliverable.

20. **"What's your revenue target for the next 12 months, and what's standing between you and hitting it?"**
    - Connects everything back to money. This is the ROI baseline.

---

## 4. TRANSITION: Prioritise Their List for Free (3-5 minutes)

**Goal:** Summarise what you heard, re-rank their priorities FOR THEM, and bridge to the pitch. This is the single most powerful trust-building move on the call.

> "Let me make sure I've got this right. Your three biggest problems are [1], [2], and [3]. Problem 1 is costing you roughly [X] per month. You've had it for [Y] months. And if you don't fix it, [consequence]. Is that fair?"

Wait for them to confirm. Then **re-prioritise for them:**

> "Okay, here is what I would do if I were you. I know problem 1 feels like the biggest one, but actually, I would start with problem 3. Here is why -- it is simpler to solve, you will see results in the first week, and it builds momentum. Once that is running, problems 1 and 2 become easier to tackle because [reason -- maybe the data feeds into it, maybe it frees up the person who handles the others, etc.]. Does that make sense?"

**Why this works (from Morningside AI's Nick Rocco, 100+ prospect calls):**
- You are giving them free strategic advice with no pitch attached
- It shows you actually LISTENED and understood their business
- It demonstrates you have done this before (you know the right order)
- They immediately see you as the expert, not the salesman
- By the end of this, they are asking YOU "how do we get started?" rather than you pitching

**Key rules:**
- Never mention yourself, your agency, or how you would build it during this section
- Just give them the direction for free
- If they have a list of 10-15 AI ideas (common with larger companies), pick the top 3 and rank them
- Always recommend starting with the EASIEST win, not the biggest one -- momentum matters more than impact at the start

> "I work with businesses like yours every day. And honestly, everything you've described -- I've seen it before, and I've fixed it before. Want me to show you what that looks like?"

---

## 5. PITCH (5 minutes)

**Goal:** Present the solution without explaining the tech. Sell the outcome, not the system.

**The MIT stat (if they need credibility):**

> "Quick bit of context. MIT published research last year showing 95% of AI projects in businesses produce zero return. The reason? They drop in generic tools without understanding the business first. We don't do that. We spend time understanding your specific problems, your specific workflows, and we build solutions that actually fit. That's why our clients see results in the first month."

**The offer:**

> "Here's what I'd suggest. We do what I call an Exploration Month. For one month, I come in, learn your business inside out, and we tackle your number one problem -- the one costing you [X] per month. By the end of the month, you'll have a working solution, a full roadmap of everything else we could automate, and real results you can see.

> The investment is two and a half thousand pounds for that first month. And here's the thing -- if at the end of the month you're not happy with where it's going, you keep everything I built, and I'll give you a full refund. Zero risk.

> If it works -- and it will -- we carry on. I solve your next two problems, and the next two after that, until your business runs itself."

**If they ask about ongoing pricing:**

> "After the Exploration Month, most clients go onto a monthly subscription. Starts at two and a half thousand a month for three core automations. As we solve more problems and add more systems, it grows from there. But let's not get ahead of ourselves -- let's prove it works first."

---

## 6. CLOSE (5 minutes)

**Goal:** Get a yes or a next step. Never leave without one.

**If they're ready:**

> "Want to get started? I can send over the contract today and we book the discovery day."

**If they need to think:**

> "Totally understand. Tell you what -- I'll send you a quick summary of what we discussed, the three problems we identified, and what the first month would look like. Take a look, talk to [partner/spouse/team], and let's reconnect on [specific day]. Does Thursday work?"

**If they push back on price:**

> "I hear you. Let me put it this way -- you told me problem 1 costs you roughly [X] per month. That's [X times 12] a year. For two and a half thousand, we either fix it or you get your money back. What's the risk?"

**If they say not right now:**

> "No problem at all. Can I do one thing for you though? Let me put together a quick report on your business -- publicly available information, nothing intrusive -- just to show you what I can see from the outside. No charge, no obligation. If it's useful, great. If not, no worries. Fair?"

Then run `/demo scanner` on their business and send them the report. That's your follow-up Trojan horse.

---

## After the Call

1. **Send the summary within 1 hour.** Recap the 3 pain points, costs, and proposed first month.
2. **Send the NDA** if not already signed.
3. **Run `/demo scanner`** on their business if you haven't already. Send the report.
4. **Log the prospect** in your pipeline.
5. **Follow up** on the agreed date. Never leave it open-ended.

## The Golden Rules

1. **Listen more than you talk.** 70/30 rule -- they talk 70%, you 20%.
2. **Never explain the tech.** "AI Operating System" means nothing to them. "I fix your problems" means everything.
3. **Sell the outcome, not the solution.** "Your pain stops" not "I build an n8n workflow."
4. **The 5 questions are non-negotiable.** Do them for every pain point.
5. **In-person beats remote.** Tyler's data: remote kickoffs don't work. Context gets lost. Be there.
6. **Show, don't tell.** The `/demo scanner` report shows more than any pitch deck.
7. **The guarantee closes every deal.** "You don't pay if you're not happy." Tyler: 100% close rate with this.
