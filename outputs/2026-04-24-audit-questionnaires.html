Part 1 — Used at every audit
Universal Discovery Questions

These five questions work for any business in any sector. They open the conversation, surface the real problems, and give you the language you need to write a proposal that lands.

1
What are the most repetitive or time-consuming tasks your team does every single day?

Listen for: data entry, chasing people, copy-pasting between systems, writing the same emails, manually booking things. These are your automation targets.

2
Where do you feel you are losing money or clients that you cannot quite explain?

Listen for: leads going quiet, slow follow-up, no-shows, enquiries that never converted, clients who stopped coming back. These are your revenue leak targets.

3
How do new customers currently find you, and what happens in the first 24 hours after they make contact?

Listen for: response time, who handles it, what system (if any) they use, what happens after hours. Slow or inconsistent first response is almost always a major gap.

4
What tools and software does your business currently use day to day?

Listen for: spreadsheets being used where software should be, multiple disconnected tools, things done manually because the team never got trained on the system they bought.

5
If you could remove one bottleneck from your business tomorrow, what would it be?

This question reveals what keeps them up at night. Whatever they say is your headline opportunity. Build your proposal around fixing this first.

Part 2 — Industry Questionnaire
Property Developer
🏗
Property Developer
Developers, builders, estate agents, new-build specialists
1
How do you currently manage new buyer enquiries, and what is your typical response time from first contact to first reply?

Listen for: manual inbox checking, slow replies, no system for out-of-hours messages. A response gap over 2 hours is a significant revenue leak in property.

2
What happens to enquiries that come in outside of business hours, on evenings and weekends?

Listen for: voicemail, no response until Monday, or a generic auto-reply with no follow-through. 35% of property searches happen outside of office hours.

3
How do you currently schedule and coordinate property viewings, and how much back-and-forth does that typically involve?

Listen for: email chains, phone tag, manually managed calendars. Every extra step in the booking process increases drop-off rate.

4
What is your process for keeping potential buyers engaged between their initial enquiry and a completed sale, particularly for longer sales cycles?

Listen for: no structured follow-up, reliance on buyers coming back by themselves, leads going cold after the first viewing.

5
How would you describe your current online presence, and how many Google reviews does your business have?

Listen for: outdated website, near-zero reviews, no social media activity. A 30-year business with 3 Google reviews has a trust gap that AI can close within 3 months.

Part 2 — Industry Questionnaire
GP Surgery
🏥
GP Surgery
GP practices, primary care, walk-in clinics, private medical
1
How many patient calls does your reception team handle per day, and roughly how many go unanswered or reach voicemail during busy periods?

Listen for: a specific number. Even "maybe 20 a day" is enough to calculate ROI. Missed calls in a GP practice carry both financial and clinical risk.

2
What percentage of your appointment slots are lost each month to late cancellations and no-shows, and what is your current process for filling them quickly?

Listen for: no automated fill system, slots going empty, staff manually calling a waiting list. NHS data shows 15 million GP appointments per year are missed in England alone.

3
How much of your reception team's time is spent on repeat prescription requests, referral letters, and routine administrative documentation?

Listen for: "most of their day" or a specific hour estimate. This is almost always the largest time sink in a GP practice and the clearest automation target.

4
Can patients currently book appointments online, and if so, what percentage actually use that route versus calling reception?

Listen for: phone still dominates, online booking exists but is underused, or no online booking at all. Digital-first booking cuts reception workload significantly.

5
What does your current patient follow-up process look like after appointments, particularly for chronic conditions requiring regular review?

Listen for: manual recall process, patients being responsible for booking their own follow-ups, or a staff member spending hours on recall letters each month.

Part 2 — Industry Questionnaire
High-Net-Worth Entrepreneur
💼
High-Net-Worth Entrepreneur
Serial founders, investors, multi-business owners, executives
1
How do you currently manage your time across multiple business interests, and where does your diary most frequently become a bottleneck?

Listen for: meetings that could be replaced by briefings, decisions routed through them unnecessarily, time spent on things no one else can do but them.

2
What decisions are you still making personally that you believe could be delegated, either to a team member or to an automated system?

Listen for: approvals on small spending, answering questions the team should know the answers to, being the only one who knows how to do something critical.

3
How do you stay across the performance of multiple businesses or investments without spending your day reading reports?

Listen for: no consolidated view, different dashboards for each business, relying on people to tell them when something goes wrong rather than seeing it themselves.

4
Where in your businesses is speed the most critical factor, and where is slowness currently costing you money or competitive advantage?

Listen for: slow proposal turnaround, slow hiring decisions, slow customer response. Speed is almost always an AI opportunity and an easy ROI conversation.

5
If your business had to run for two weeks without you, what would break first, and why?

This question reveals every manual dependency and bottleneck that sits with them personally. Whatever they name first is your highest-value AI opportunity.

Part 2 — Industry Questionnaire
Dental Practice
🦷
Dental Practice
Dental surgeries, orthodontists, cosmetic dental clinics
1
How many new patient enquiry calls does your reception team miss per day, including calls during busy periods, lunch, and after hours?

Listen for: any number above zero. UK dental data shows practices miss 50 to 100 new patient calls per month. That is £60,000 to £120,000 in lost annual revenue at average treatment values.

2
What percentage of your appointment slots are lost each month to late cancellations and no-shows, and what does your current process for filling them quickly look like?

Listen for: slots going empty, manual waitlist calls, no automated reminder system. A 10% no-show rate on 200 monthly appointments is 20 empty slots every month.

3
How do you currently request Google reviews from patients after treatment, and how consistent is that process across your team?

Listen for: it depends on who remembers to ask, no system at all, or a paper card that rarely gets used. Online reviews are the number one trust signal for new dental patients.

4
How do you manage recall appointments for patients who need to come back in 6 or 12 months, and how many of those patients actually return?

Listen for: manual letters, hoping patients remember, or a recall rate significantly below 70%. Each lapsed recall patient represents a predictable annual revenue loss.

5
How much of your reception team's day is spent on tasks that feel administrative rather than patient-facing, and what takes up most of that time?

Listen for: confirming appointments by phone, chasing outstanding treatment plans, insurance paperwork, writing the same correspondence repeatedly.

Part 2 — Industry Questionnaire
Travel Agency
✈
Travel Agency
Travel agents, tour operators, luxury travel consultants, booking services
1
How do you currently handle new enquiries that come in outside of business hours, including website contact forms, social media messages, and phone calls on evenings and weekends?

Listen for: nothing until Monday, an auto-reply with no follow-up plan, or a consultant personally checking their phone. Travel enquiries are highly time-sensitive, especially for time-specific deals.

2
What does your current process look like for creating and sending a travel proposal to a client, from the moment they enquire to the moment they receive a quote?

Listen for: hours of manual research, copy-pasting between platforms, formatting PDFs by hand. A quote that takes 3 hours to produce could be reduced to 20 minutes with the right AI tools.

3
How do you keep past clients engaged and encourage repeat bookings, whether through newsletters, personalised offers, or direct follow-up?

Listen for: no structured re-engagement, the occasional email blast, or relying entirely on clients coming back by themselves. Repeat clients in travel are 5x cheaper to convert than new ones.

4
What is your biggest administrative bottleneck right now: supplier communication, booking admin, client documentation, or something else entirely?

Listen for: a specific task that takes disproportionate time. In travel agencies, supplier confirmation chasing and itinerary building are consistently the top two time sinks.

5
If you could clone yourself or your best consultant and have that person working 24 hours a day, what would you have them doing first?

This surfaces the highest-value task that is currently constrained by human availability. Whatever they say is almost certainly something AI can replicate or support.
