About this session

This is a structured discovery session. The goal is to understand how your business currently operates across seven key areas: speaking, the Academy, content and social media, the Foundation, your time and operations, your technology setup, and your financial admin.

There are no right or wrong answers. The more honest you are, the more useful the output. By the end of this session, we will have a clear picture of where AI and automation can save you the most time and generate the most value.

🎤
The Speaking Business
Inquiry management, admin overhead, revenue, lost opportunities
Q1
Walk me through what happens when someone wants to book you to speak. From the moment they reach out to the day you step on stage. What does that process look like?
Q2
In the last 12 months, do you feel like you missed any speaking opportunities because they came in and did not get responded to quickly enough, or because the back-and-forth just took too long?
Q3
On average, how many hours per week does speaking coordination take up? This includes emails, contracts, briefs, logistics, prep calls, all of it.
Q4
You are represented by Champions Speakers. How much of your speaking comes through them versus direct inquiries to your website or email? And what happens to the direct ones?
Q5
What does a typical keynote fee look like for you? Just a ballpark. I want to understand the value of a single booking so I can show you what missing even two per year costs.
🎓
The Louding Voice Academy
Enrollment funnel, email sequences, conversion, course operations
Q6
Tell me about The Louding Voice Academy. Who is it for, what does it teach, and how do people typically find their way to it?
Q7
You have a newsletter called The Louding Life. How many subscribers are on that list? And are you actively trying to move those readers toward the Academy?
Q8
When someone signs up to your newsletter, what happens next? Do they get a welcome sequence, a series of emails, an introduction to the Academy? Or do they just go straight onto the general list?
Q9
How much time do you personally put into Academy admin each month? Enrollment processing, student communications, course updates. Is any of it automated?
📱
Content and Social Media
Time spent, consistency, platforms, audience growth
Q10
How much time do you currently spend creating content for Instagram, Twitter, and your newsletter combined? Be honest. I want to understand the real cost.
Q11
You have 184,000 people who rated your book on Goodreads. You have 12,000 Instagram followers. That is a 15 to 1 ratio. In your view, why is that gap so wide and does it bother you?
Q12
Have you ever considered video content, YouTube, or TikTok? You are a compelling speaker. BookTok has made careers for authors with far smaller audiences than yours. What has held you back?
Q13
You are published in 24 languages. Do you have any content strategy for international audiences, particularly in markets like Nigeria, where your story resonates most? Or is it purely through the publisher?
❤️
The Louding Voice Foundation
Donor pipeline, supporter nurture, admin, school visits
Q14
Tell me about The Louding Voice Foundation. How does it currently acquire supporters and donors? Is there an active pipeline or is it mainly word of mouth from your events and speaking appearances?
Q15
School and organisation visits are part of the Foundation's work. How are those currently booked and coordinated? What does the admin look like from an inquiry to a confirmed visit?
Q16
If the Foundation had a system that automatically nurtured every person who engaged with it online or at an event into an active supporter, what would that change for the Foundation's impact?
⏰
Time, Operations and Admin
What your days actually look like, where the time goes
Q17
If I asked you to describe a typical week, what does it look like? Where does the time actually go across writing, speaking, Academy, Foundation, and everything else?
Q18
Do you have a personal assistant or a team? If yes, what do they handle? If no, what would you hire for first if you had the budget?
Q19
What would you do with 20 extra hours every month? If admin were not taking up those hours, where would they go?
Q20
When you think about press coverage, new reviews, award nominations, or media mentions, how do you currently find out about them? Is it a system or is it when someone tags you or sends you a message?
💻
Technology and Current Setup
What you already use, what you know, where the gaps are
Q21
What email marketing tool are you using for The Louding Life newsletter? Mailchimp, ConvertKit, something else? And are you actively using any of the automation features in it?
Q22
Do you use any CRM or pipeline tool to track speaking inquiries, corporate contacts, or Academy prospects? Anything to manage relationships and follow-ups?
Q23
How do you feel about AI generally? Are you using it at all in your work currently, even informally?
📄
Tax and Financial Administration
Income tracking, expenses, Self Assessment prep, accountant relationship
Q24
How many separate income streams do you have right now? Walk me through them. Book royalties, speaking fees, Academy income, Foundation, anything else.
Q25
Do you file a Self Assessment tax return with HMRC? And do you do it yourself or does an accountant handle it?
Q26
How do you currently track your business expenses? Receipts in a folder, a spreadsheet, an app, or does it all come out at the end of the year in a panic?
Q27
What kinds of expenses run through your business? Travel, home office, equipment, subscriptions, professional fees, event costs, anything related to the Foundation?
Q28
Do you have a business bank account that is fully separate from your personal account? Or does business income and spending mix with personal at any point?
Q29
You are published in 24 languages. Do you receive royalties from publishers in different countries? And if so, how does that get handled when it comes to tax? Is your accountant managing the international side?
Q30
How long does tax preparation actually take you each year? From start to finish, how many hours would you say go into getting everything together for your return?
Q31
Are you VAT registered, or are any of your income streams subject to VAT? And if so, are you managing VAT returns separately?
