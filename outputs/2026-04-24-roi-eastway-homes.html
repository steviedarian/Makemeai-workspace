Executive Summary

Eastway Homes is a trusted Essex property developer with 30 years of trading history and a strong local reputation. The problem is not the product. The problem is the pipeline. Enquiries are being missed after hours. Viewings are being lost to slow scheduling. Leads that showed interest six months ago have never been followed up. There are almost no online reviews, no consistent social presence, and no automated way to capture or convert digital interest. This analysis puts a conservative annual cost on those gaps: £371,000 every year the current approach continues.

The top opportunity is after-hours lead capture. Property buyers browse evenings and weekends. Without a 24/7 response system, those enquiries go cold before anyone picks up on Monday morning. Based on conservative estimates of 15 monthly enquiries, 35% landing out of hours, and recovering just one sale per year, that single gap represents £167,300 in annual value. The second largest opportunity is automated viewing scheduling: friction in the booking process costs Eastway an estimated £83,650 per year in dropped viewings and delayed sales cycles.

Across all five opportunities, the total year-one investment to fix these problems is £16,400, split between build costs and annual tool licences. The net return in year one is £354,600. Every figure in this report has been calculated conservatively, using the low end of industry benchmarks. The intangibles are not counted: faster response times, reduced staff burnout, a consistent brand presence, and the ability to scale without hiring.
