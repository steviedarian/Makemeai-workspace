Full Script — 2.0 to 2.5 Minutes
Read twice, then speak naturally. This is a guide, not a teleprompter.
[Settle, smile, direct to camera]

"Hello. My name is Abi Dare. I am the author of The Girl with the Louding Voice, a New York Times bestseller that has been translated into 24 languages and won the Bath Novel Award. I am also a speaker. I teach a writing course. And I run a global foundation that funds girls' education across the world.

So as you can imagine, I have a lot going on at the same time.

[Small laugh, genuine]

And with all of that comes a lot of everything else. Emails. Calls. Inquiries. Contracts to review. Invoices to send. Followers to respond to. Students to support. Events to prepare for. Grant applications to write. And on top of all of that, I still need to actually write my next book.

It is a lot. And for a long time, I just accepted that this was what being busy looked like.

[Shift tone, more reflective]

But when Olusegun sat with me and went through everything, question by question, I realised... it wasn't just busy. It was scattered. He showed me, very clearly, that I had contracts sitting in my inbox unsigned, money I hadn't invoiced, newsletter subscribers I hadn't spoken to in months, and royalties I hadn't chased in two years.

[ Pause. Let it land. ]

Two years. Money that belongs to me. Just sitting there.

And I'd just... accepted that as life. I didn't realise those things were fixable. Or that there was someone who could come in, look at the whole picture, and say 'here is where you're losing time and money, and here's exactly what we're going to do about it.'

That's what Olusegun did. He didn't just point it out. He quantified it. He mapped it. He showed me what it was costing me to run my business the way I was running it.

And now we're building systems together. Proper ones. So that things get caught before they fall. So I wake up knowing what needs doing. So the people who want to work with me actually hear from me.

I'm really pleased with where this is going. He gets it. He understands what I need, even when I struggle to articulate it. And I genuinely feel for the first time that someone has a proper handle on my business alongside me.

[Lean in slightly, direct to camera]

And my honest recommendation? It doesn't matter how big your organisation is or how small. Whether you're a solo person like me juggling five things at once, or a company with a whole team. You have no idea what MakeMeAI can do for you until you sit down with them. I was completely blown away when I saw, in black and white, the amount I had been throwing away, and how simple, targeted AI tools are now helping me to recover those losses and get my business properly organised.

Talk to MakeMeAI. Have that conversation. You will see for yourself."

Director's Notes for Abi

  • Film somewhere with good natural light, ideally near a window. No studio setup needed.
  • The opening introduction should feel proud and warm, not like a CV recitation. She has earned those credentials.
  • The small laugh after listing everything she does should be genuine, not performed. It tells the audience she knows how much it is.
  • The pause after "Two years" is the most important moment in the script. Do not rush past it.
  • On the final recommendation, lean forward slightly or shift energy. That contrast makes the call to action land harder.
  • She can say "Segun" instead of "Olusegun" if that is what she calls him naturally. Authenticity over formality.
  • One take is fine. A real, slightly imperfect delivery will always outperform a polished but stiff one.
